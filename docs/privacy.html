CastForge Privacy Policy

_Last updated: 2026-04-24_

CastForge is a desktop streaming application that runs entirely on your own computer. This page explains what data CastForge touches, where it lives, who sees it, and how you can remove it.

One-line summary: CastForge stores all account data locally on your machine, encrypted by your operating system. Nothing is sent to a CastForge server, because there isn't one.

1. Who runs CastForge

CastForge is developed and distributed by the CastForge project. Contact: . This policy covers the CastForge desktop application and any associated Meta Platforms / Facebook app registrations used to enable the "Connect Facebook" feature inside CastForge.

2. What data CastForge accesses

When you connect a Facebook account inside CastForge, Facebook's OAuth flow grants CastForge the following scopes:

  • pages_show_list — the list of Facebook Pages you administer
  • pages_read_engagement — comments and reactions on your own Pages, so

    • CastForge can display them in the on-screen chat overlay

  • pages_manage_metadata — metadata about your own Pages (used for

    • stream setup and titling)

From these scopes CastForge reads, in memory:

  • Your Facebook user ID and display name (shown in the app UI so you can

    • see which account is connected)

  • The list of Pages you administer (so you can pick which one to stream

    • to)

  • Public comments posted to livestreams on Pages you administer (so the

    • chat overlay can display them)

CastForge does not request, read, or store:

  • Your Facebook password
  • Your private messages, friend list, birthday, phone number, or any

    • other profile data beyond ID and display name

  • Comments, posts, or data from anyone else's Pages or accounts
  • Any data unrelated to the streams you initiate inside CastForge

3. Where the data lives

All data CastForge touches is processed locally on your computer.

  • OAuth access tokens are encrypted using your operating system's

    • credential vault via Electron's safeStorage API. On Windows that is DPAPI (keyed to your Windows user account). On macOS that is Keychain. On Linux that is libsecret (GNOME Keyring / KWallet). The encrypted blob is written to a file inside the CastForge app-data directory, with file-mode 0600 where the OS supports it.

  • Page lists, user display names, and live comments are held in

    • memory only. They are not written to disk and they disappear when you close CastForge.

CastForge does not operate any backend server. No CastForge-controlled server receives your Facebook token, your comments, your viewer list, your Page data, or any other personal information. Your traffic goes directly from your computer to Meta / Facebook over HTTPS.

4. Who we share data with

Nobody. CastForge has no server to share from, no analytics SDK, no third-party telemetry, no ad network, and no data-broker integration.

Your streaming output (video, audio, overlays) is sent directly from your machine to the destination you choose (e.g. Facebook Live, YouTube, Twitch, Kick). Those destinations' own privacy policies govern what they do with it once it arrives.

5. How long data is kept

  • OAuth tokens persist on your disk until you disconnect the account in

    • CastForge, uninstall CastForge, or your OS credential vault purges them.

  • In-memory data (Page list, comments, display name) is dropped when

    • CastForge exits.

There is no remote copy to expire, because there is no remote copy.

6. How to revoke access or delete data

You control your data. Any of the following will remove CastForge's access:

1. Inside the app: open CastForge, go to the Facebook tile on the dashboard, and click "Disconnect". This clears the local encrypted token file immediately. 2. On Facebook: visit , locate "CastForge", and click "Remove". Facebook will invalidate any tokens the app currently holds. 3. Uninstall CastForge: removing the application (and its user-data directory) deletes the local encrypted token file along with every other local artefact.

Because CastForge stores nothing on a remote server, options 1-3 are sufficient to delete 100% of what CastForge has ever had. There is no support request to file and no deletion queue to wait on.

7. Children

CastForge is a tool for livestreamers and is not directed at children under 13. Do not use CastForge if you are under 13.

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at the same URL with an updated "Last updated" date at the top.
